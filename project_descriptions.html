This page showcases some of the research work I did as a scientist at RAND

Below are project descriptions, visualizations, and links for eight projects, out of dozens I worked on between 2014 and 2020. Here is the one-sentence description of my work on each:

  • Conducted app black box testing and developed metrics to support a $1.2 billion disaster mitigation grant program

Conducted app black box testing and developed metrics to support a $1.2 billion disaster
mitigation grant program; client used research to win OMB approval to launch the program

Client objective: Establish operating procedure for a new grant program and win OMB approval to launch it.

Project objective: Formulate metrics to support program decision-making procedure and conduct black-box testing of the program's core project scoring app.

Leadership role: Led a nine-member project team, organized as three task teams working in parallel. Mentored junior staff extensively on metrics design and did quality reviews on all project products.

Technical role: Personally calculated cost driver statistics (Appendix A) and conducted black box testing on the program's core app (not included in the project report).

Client outcome: Used research to win OMB approval and formulate program policy. Delighted with the project's products and purchased a million dollars in follow-on work.

Project products (click to view): Project report, actionable recommendations, and decision support scorecards (x2).

Facilitated a disaster recovery plan as an on-the-ground liaison for
the plan writers; client used the plan to obtain $80 billion from Congress

Client objective: Following the worst natural disaster in 90 years, Congress required the Puerto Rican government to write a disaster recovery plan within 180 days as a condition of receiving funding to rebuild.

Project objective: Compile multi-stakeholder input into a comprehensive recovery plan; estimate the plan's total cost.

Liaison role: Relocated to San Juan for six months as one of twelve liaisons representing the plan-writing team. Liaised with Puerto Rican cabinet-level officials and 21 stakeholder organizations/agencies. Negotiated data access, facilitated cooperation, and provided analysis to the plan-writing team.

Technical role: Improvised numerous solutions to enable team success despite data availability/quality challenges and a fast, congressionally-mandated deadline. For example, wrote code to extract data from PDF field reports and compile it into a clean dataset suitable for analysis.

Client outcome: Congress provided $80 billion in recovery assistance; the plan continues to guide rebuilding efforts.

Project products (click to view): Co-authored reports informing the plan (x2) and the plan itself.

Simulated policy impact using NCANDS datasets to make the child welfare system more
effective; client used research to lobby successfully for the Families First Prevention Act

Client objective: Pass legislation allowing Federal Title IV-E funding to be used for family preservation services and child abuse prevention instead of only funding foster care services.

Project objective: Build a simulation of the child welfare system and simulate the impact of various policy proposals.

Technical role: Served as lead programmer, building the simulation from scratch and simulating policy scenarios.

Client outcome: Research supported client's efforts to lobby for the Family First Prevention Services Act, which became law in 2018.

Project products (click to view): Project report, technical appendix, and simulation schematics (x3).

Applied unsupervised machine learning to personnel records to derive an empirically-grounded
career path typology; client used research to implement a new talent management system

Client objective: Be more successful at promoting from within to produce the next generation of senior leadership.

Task objective: Examine the career histories of past senior leadership; develop a typology of common career paths culminating in senior positions.

Leadership role: Executed this task as part of a larger project. Was lead author for the project-wide technical report.

Technical role: Applied a custom blend of sequence analysis and network analysis community detection to develop a typology of senior leader career histories. Identified "missing rungs" on typical employee career progression paths. Typology accurately described over 80 percent of senior leader histories with just four categories.

Client outcome: Client implemented a new talent management program with streamlined career progression pathways, and an app that recommends career next steps / advancement opportunities to interested employees.

Project products: Client asked that the report (PR-4388-A) and technical appendix (PR-4388/1-A) not be released publicly, since they discuss sensitive personnel policy matters.

Performed statistical analysis and A/B testing to understand social media engagement; client
used research to make social media content more gender-inclusive, which increased audience engagement

Client objective: Use social media accounts to increase public enthusiasm and meet new employee recruitment goals.

Task objective: Analyze audience engagement with a specific social media account.

Leadership role: Worked independently on this task as part of a larger project.

Technical role: Performed statistical analysis on the acount's engagement metrics across years of content. Designed an A/B testing plan to determine if making content more gender-inclusive would improve engagement.

Client outcome: A/B testing revealed that inclusivity boosted engagement and the client changed their content generation process accordingly.

Project products (click to view): Project report (see pages 18-30) and examples of the four most engaging content types (see pages 21-22).

Employed unsupervised machine learning on voter survey data to analyze presidential candidate support; wrote articles geared towards the public that attracted more traffic to client's website than any other page that year

Client objective: Contribute to client's public profile.

Project objective: Use client's flagship longitudinal survey to produce analysis articles on the presidential election.

Leadership role: Co-led project. Was lead author on half of the articles produced.

Technical role: Cleaned and analyzed data for each new wave as it came in. Generated data-based visualizations to make the analyses more intuitive.

Technical contribution example: Applied principal component analysis to voter ideology questions to make a map of the "electoral terrain" and then charted each candidate's support as "territory" on that map.

Client outcome: Election analysis articles drove more traffic to client's website than any other page that year and were cited in over eighty news media articles.

Product projects (click to view): "Electoral terrain" articles (x3) and data visualizations (x2).

Developed supervised machine learning procedure using social media to measure
ideological prevalence; used to measure uptake of Russian propaganda in Eastern Europe

Client objective: Measure support for propagandist ideology in a social media population.

Project objective: Provide a rigorous validation study of a previously developed procedure for measuring ideological prevalence on social media.

Context: Wrote the code developing the method in two previous projects on countering social media propagandists.

Leadership role: Led a four-person team conducting the validation study.

Technical role: Wrote all of the code implementing the procedure, gathering/shaping the data used for validation, and executing the validation tests. Crafted data visualizations to showcase results.

Validation outcome: Procedure performed excellently during testing (precision = 0.95, recall = 0.95), providing strong empirical support for expanding field use of the procedure.

Project products (click to view): Validation project report, propaganda project report (Chp. 4), and visualizations (x3).

Developed text mining procedure using social media to detect/extract
information about chemical weapons incidents as they happen

Client objective: Detect chemical weapons incidents and extract information about them, even if they occur in environments denied to western media and intelligence.

Project objective: Develop a hybrid computational/analyst procedure for using social media to detect and gain information about chemical weapons incidents.

Leadership role: Co-led a seven-member project team, divided into computational and analyst procedure development task teams. Served as lead author on the project report.

Technical role: Developed, programmed, and tested the computational procedure, which detected chemical weapons incidents by finding surges in emotive language.

Project products (click to view): Project report and method overview diagrams (x3).
